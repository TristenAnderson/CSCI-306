Assignment 1

50 points

This assignment is in three parts:

Part A

Write a program that does the following:

  • Declare a fullword FIRST with the value 219.
  • Declare a fullword SECOND with the value 83.
  • Add SECOND to FIRST. To do this, simply load these numbers into registers and use an AR.
  • Subtract SECOND from FIRST. To do this, load the two numbers into registers (a different pair of registers) and use an SR.
  • Now use an XDUMP to dump out the registers and look at the register values to see your results.

Part B

Modify the program from Part A as follows:

  • Declare two consecutive fullwords of storage called SUM and DIFF.
  • Store (ST) the sum (FIRST plus SECOND) into SUM.
  • Store (ST) the difference (FIRST minus SECOND) into DIFF.
  • XDUMP that area of storage (containing SUM and DIFF) using XDUMP. You can use XDUMP to print the values in storage by using the label followed by the length of the field like this: 
         XDUMP SUM,8

    or maybe as two lines: 

         XDUMP SUM,4 
     XDUMP DIFF,4
  • Look at the XDUMP output and verify your arithmetic is correct.

Part C

Now rewrite your program using explicit addressing. This means on the L and ST, take out your labels and fill in the addresses of the various fullwords. You can get these addresses from the program listing of part B (in a column at the left).

Other Notes
